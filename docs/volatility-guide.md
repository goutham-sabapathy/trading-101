# Volatility Guide for Earnings Trading

Understanding volatility is critical.

---

# Types of Volatility

1. Historical Volatility (HV)
   - Based on past price movement

2. Implied Volatility (IV)
   - Market expectation of future move

---

# Earnings Volatility Behavior

Typical pattern:

- IV rises 2–3 weeks before earnings
- IV peaks 1 day before earnings
- IV collapses immediately after earnings

This collapse is called:
IV Crush

---

# IV Rank

IV Rank = Where current IV sits relative to past 1-year range.

High IV Rank (>70):
→ Selling premium favored

Low IV Rank (<30):
→ Buying premium favored

---

# Key Insight

Volatility traders do NOT predict direction.

They trade:
- Volatility expansion
- Volatility contraction
